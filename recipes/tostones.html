Tostones

Shown: Tostones

Tostones are a popular dish in Latin American and Caribbean cuisine, consisting of twice-fried green plantain slices. They are known for their savory, crispy texture and are often served as a side dish or appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 1 Green Plantain
  • 5 Tablespoons of Frying Oil
  • 3 Cups of Cold Water
  • Salt to Taste

Step by Step

  1. Peel plantain and cut into 1-inch slices. Fill a bowl with 3 cups of cold water.
  2. Heat oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat; add plantain slices in an even layer and fry on both sides until golden brown, about 3 1/2 minutes per side. Set skillet aside.
  3. Transfer plantain slices to a chopping board; flatten each one by placing a small plate on top and pressing down.
  4. Dip plantain slices in cold water.
  5. Reheat oil in the skillet over medium heat; cook plantain slices for 1 minute on each side.
  6. Season to taste with salt and serve immediately.
